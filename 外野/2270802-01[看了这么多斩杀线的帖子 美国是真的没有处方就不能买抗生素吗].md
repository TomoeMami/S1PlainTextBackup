This strict regulation is in place to prevent misuse, which can contribute to the rise of antibiotic-resistant bacteria—one of the most pressing global health concerns today. Why does this matter? Antibiotics are powerful medications, but they’re only effective against bacterial infections. They don’t work against viruses, which are often the culprits behind illnesses like the common cold or the flu. Using antibiotics improperly can lead to serious consequences for both individual patients and public health as a whole. This is why access to antibiotics in the U.S. is tightly controlled. While having to visit a healthcare provider for a prescription may seem like an inconvenience, it’s a safeguard designed to ensure that antibiotics are used correctly and only when absolutely necessary.
