By Reuters

March 16, 2026 11:48 AM GMT+8

SINGAPORE, March 16 (Reuters) - China's Hua Hong Group has developed advanced chip manufacturing technologies that can be used to produce artificial intelligence chips, four people familiar with the matter said, a major milestone in Beijing's efforts to boost tech self-sufficiency.

The group's ​contract chipmaking business, Huali Microelectronics, is readying a 7-nanometre (nm) chipmaking process at its plant in Shanghai, the people said, which would ‌make it the second Chinese chipmaker with such advanced technologies. Hua Hong is China's second-largest chipmaker.
